Getting Started

What do I need to run TradingBrainz?
System Requirements:
  • Windows 10/11 (64-bit)
  • Python 3.10 or higher
  • 8GB RAM minimum (16GB recommended for local transcription)
  • 5GB free disk space
Required API Keys (you provide):
  • Anthropic - For Claude AI strategy extraction
  • QuantConnect - For backtesting (free tier available)
  • Tradovate - For live bot trading (if using bots)
How much do the API keys cost?
  • Anthropic (Claude) - Usage-based pricing. Typical usage for strategy extraction is $5-20/month depending on how much content you process.
  • QuantConnect - Free tier includes backtesting. Paid tiers ($8-24/mo) for more backtests and features.
  • Tradovate - Free API access with a funded account. Standard broker commissions apply to trades.
Can I use a different AI besides Claude?
The system is natively built and optimized for Anthropic's Claude. At purchase, you can specify if you prefer a different AI provider, and we'll configure the system accordingly. However, Claude provides the best results for strategy extraction due to its strong reasoning capabilities.
Is TradingBrainz Mac or Linux compatible?
Currently, TradingBrainz is Windows-only. The capture system uses Windows-specific APIs for audio recording and OCR. Mac/Linux support may be added in future updates, but there's no timeline for this.

Technical Questions

Is my data stored locally or in the cloud?
All your data is stored locally on your machine:
  • Transcripts and captures → SQLite database on your PC
  • Trading strategies and setup cards → Local files
  • Trade history and journal → Local JSON files
The only cloud interactions are API calls to Anthropic (AI processing), QuantConnect (backtesting), and your broker (trade execution). We don't have access to your strategies or trading data.
Does TradingBrainz work with NinjaTrader?
Yes. TradingBrainz includes NinjaTrader integration for receiving signals. You can use NT8 as your charting platform and signal source, with TradingBrainz handling the execution through Tradovate. This is useful for traders who have existing NinjaTrader strategies.
What markets can I trade?
TradingBrainz is designed for futures trading through Tradovate. Commonly traded instruments include:
  • ES (E-mini S&P 500)
  • NQ (E-mini Nasdaq)
  • CL (Crude Oil)
  • GC (Gold)
  • And other Tradovate-supported futures
Stock, forex, and crypto trading are not currently supported.
How does the AI strategy extraction work?
When you end a capture session:
  1. Audio is transcribed locally using Whisper
  2. Screenshots are processed with OCR
  3. All content is sent to Claude AI for analysis
  4. Claude extracts indicators, conditions, entry/exit rules, and risk parameters
  5. Results are formatted into structured "setup cards"
  6. Setup cards can be converted to QuantConnect algorithm code
The quality of extraction depends on the clarity of the source material. Well-structured courses with explicit rules produce better results than vague or ambiguous content.

Usage Questions

Is this a signals service or copy trading?
No. TradingBrainz is a toolkit, not a signals service. We don't provide:
  • Trading signals or alerts
  • Pre-built strategies
  • Trade recommendations
  • Copy trading from other users
You develop your own strategies using the Brain's capture and extraction tools. You're responsible for what you trade and how you trade it.
Will TradingBrainz make me profitable?
We make no guarantees about profitability. TradingBrainz is a tool that helps you:
  • Capture and organize trading knowledge
  • Extract rules from educational content
  • Backtest strategies before risking real money
  • Execute with discipline and compliance
Your results depend entirely on the strategies you develop, the quality of your education sources, and your ability to manage risk. The tool doesn't create profitable strategies - it helps you develop and execute your own.
How long does it take to learn the system?
Most users can complete basic setup and run their first capture session within an hour. The full workflow (capture → extract → backtest → execute) typically takes a few days to become comfortable with. We include documentation and guides to help you get started.
